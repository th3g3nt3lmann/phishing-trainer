How to Protect Against Phishing

Essential tips to keep your information safe

Check the Sender's Email Address

Always verify the sender's email address carefully. Legitimate companies use their official domain names (e.g., @netflix.com, @amazon.com). Be suspicious of slight variations like @netflix-account.com or @amazon-security.net.

Inspect Links Before Clicking

Hover over links to preview the URL. Ensure it matches the official website exactly. Don't click on shortened URLs or links that look suspicious. When in doubt, manually type the website address in your browser.

Don't Fall for Urgency

Be skeptical of emails that create a sense of urgency or fear. Phishers often use threats of account suspension or security breaches to make you act quickly without thinking. Take your time to verify the authenticity.

Never Share Sensitive Information

Legitimate companies will never ask for sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or social security numbers via email. Always use official websites or apps for any account-related actions.
